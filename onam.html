Onam - Festival of Kerala

Onam is the biggest festival of Kerala, celebrated with great joy and unity. It is a harvest festival that marks the homecoming of King Mahabali. Onam usually falls in the Malayalam month of Chingam (August-September).

ONAM is the harvest festival of Kerala, celebrated to welcome King Mahabali. It is marked with beautiful flower carpets (Pookalam), grand feasts (Onam Sadhya), boat races, and traditional dances. The festival shows the culture, unity, and joy of the people of Kerala.

Why is it celebrated?
According to legend, King Mahabali was a kind and generous ruler. His return is celebrated every year as Onam, symbolizing prosperity and equality. People believe that King Mahabali visits Kerala during Onam to see his people.
