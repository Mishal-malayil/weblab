Holi - The Festival of Colors

Holi is one of the most joyful and colorful festivals celebrated in India. It marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. People celebrate Holi by playing with colors, singing, dancing, and enjoying festive sweets.

Why is it celebrated?
Holi is linked to the legend of Prahlad and Holika, symbolizing the triumph of devotion and goodness over evil. It is also a time to forgive, forget, and strengthen relationships. HOLI is the festival of colors, celebrated across India with great joy and energy. People play by throwing colored powders and water, sing and dance, and share festive sweets like gujiya. It marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil, bringing people together in happiness and unity.
